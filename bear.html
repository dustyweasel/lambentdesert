THE GRIZZLY BEAR WHO SLEPT UNTIL ALL HIS PROBLEMS WENT AWAY
-a children's tale by: laconiclizard

Mr. Grizzly Bear and all his friends met in the middle of the forest. Winter was coming and everyone was very worried. "What will we eat?" said Mr. Rabbit. "All of the grass will die!"

"What will we drink?" said Mr. Moose. "The river will freeze!"

"How will we stay warm?" said Mr. Fox. "The wind will freeze us to the bones!"

All of these problems made Mr. Grizzly Bear very worried. So worried that he went back to his cave, layed down, buried his face in his paws, and tried to stop thinking about all the things that bothered him. He had almost fallen asleep when little Miss Mouse found him and gave him a good scolding. "Mr. Grizzly Bear, you can't just go to sleep and ignore your problems. When you wake up your problems will still be there and they will be even worse than before!"

"Whatever," said Mr. Grizzly Bear. "I don't want to think about any of that."

So Mr. Grizzly bear went to sleep. And he slept. And he slept. And he slept and slept and slept. Days passed and he slept. Weeks passed and he slept. Mr. Grizzly Bear slept for months. When he finally woke up he went outside and the sun was shining, the air was warm, the grass was green, the river was flowing, and there was plenty to eat. As Mr. Grizzly bear yawned and cleared his head he remembered how little Miss Mouse said he couldn't just sleep until all his problems went away. But he totally could and it was awesome.

THE END
